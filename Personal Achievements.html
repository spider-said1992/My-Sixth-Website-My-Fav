INTRODUCTION

Your definition of success is unique to you. In this worksheet, we’ll tackle just how to pinpoint the areas in your life that seem to be holding you back from your success.

We do this by first outlining what you really want in life. Next, we’ll dig deeper into what’s preventing you from getting what you want.

Each question's answer will be relevant to the following question, so please complete them in the order given.

THE CHECKLIST

Answer each question in the boxes provided
  1. What does success look like to you?
  2. What would it take for you to feel successful?

    3. This is not the time to be modest—really think about how your life would be different if you were reaching your full potential

  3. What's on your bucket list?

    4. Most of us have "bucket lists". These are lists of goals that we'd like to accomplish in our lifetime.

  4. Think of a goal that you want to accomplish. Why do you want to accomplish this goal? How will your life improve if you accomplish this goal?
  5. What do you think is holding you back from reaching these goals?

    6. In general, there are two types of potential blockers: Internal - limiting beliefs about your self or your potential and External - actual conditions in your environment that prevent you from reaching your potential

    reaching your potential

  6. Are you good at remembering things?

    7. E.g. birthdays, appointment and meeting times, information you have read

  7. How is your time management? Do you often feel rushed or bored?
  8. What is your energy level like? Are there certain things that motivate you? What are they?
  9. Do you treat your body and mind kindly? What types of things are you doing to take proper care of yourself?
  10. Who do you look up to? What successes do these people enjoy that you would like to enjoy for yourself?
  11. What are your greatest strengths?

    12. Include all your strengths, in work and your personal life.

  12. List out the all things in your life that you would like to change

    13. Examples could be getting more exercise, making more money, having more time in your day, feeling less tired... list everything you can think of

Now that you've finished filling out your answers. Reflect deeply on what you've written. Feel free to revise as much as needed--this need not be a quick exercise.

The longer you spend answering these questions, the more you may find you reveal about your own hopes, dreams, and desires.
